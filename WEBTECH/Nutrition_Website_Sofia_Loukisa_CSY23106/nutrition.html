Diet quality and
academic performance

Food plays a crucial role in students life for learning and ensuring their overall health to attend college regularly. The nutritional value of food directly impacts brain development, memory, cognitive function, energy levels, attention span, and focus, all of which are essential for absorbing and retaining information effectively. Students who maintain a balanced diet are more likely to exhibit improved school attendance, achieve better grades, and attain higher test scores compared to those with poor dietary habits.

College students who face food insecurity are more prone to experiencing academic setbacks. They are more likely to struggle with assignments and exams, withdraw from classes or even drop out of university, and achieve lower grade point averages compared to their peers. Moreover, they also report missing out on valuable professional development opportunities, such as internships, which can significantly impact their future career aspirations.

Diet and well-being

Proper nutrition plays an essential role in the mental well-being of individuals. Insufficient access to nutritious food can have detrimental effects on the mental health of college students, hindering their ability to learn and thrive. Conversely, when their nutritional needs are adequately met, students can prioritize their overall wellness.

Diet and social life

The impact of social life on students diet is significant. When they are in the company of others, their eating habits tend to change compared to when they dine alone. Additionally, dietary preferences often align with those of our close social circle. This can be attributed to the fact that conforming to the behavior of others is advantageous and brings us a sense of fulfillment.

College students face numerous challenges in their daily lives. They must juggle attending classes, working, exercising, and maintaining a social life, leaving little time for anything else. Among these activities, finding time to eat becomes an additional hurdle that often proves to be more difficult than anticipated. However, it is very important for all college students to grasp the concept of healthy food strategies and recognize the significance of maintaining a balanced diet. By doing so, they can ensure that they consume nutritious meals that provide them with the necessary fuel to sustain their energy levels throughout the day.
